Fan comics are comics that rely on worlds set up by pre-existing media. Comics with an asterisk* are not technically fan works, but rather original reinterpretations of classical texts (such as jekyll/hyde and the phantom of the opera). Comics labeled with an asterisk* will also appear on other lists as well. Comics will also have a tag to label them as Chill, Nasty, or Queer to indicate where they generally land on that scale.

Summaries here will be written my me unless stated otherwise and may not reflect how the authors would describe their work.

Doodle Duck Comics by Sarah Jolley



A series of fan comics mainly focused on Gladstone Gander from Ducktales. Genuinely some of the best storytelling I've ever read. My favourites are "The End of the Rainbow" and "The White Balloon"

A Tale Of Two Rulers by Figment Forms



Legend Of Zelda: Zelda and Ganondorft do an arranged marriage but then fall in love. It's cute.

Mokepon by H0lyhandgrenade



Mokepon is a fancomic about a jaded artist in the pokemon universe who hates pokemon and pokemon battling but is forced to go on a long and annoying journey about it anyway.

*The Glass Scientists by Sage Cotugno



A queer retelling of Strange Case of Dr Jekyll and Mr Hyde.

*Fantome-stein by Beka Duke



A retelling of the phantom of the opera, except the phantom is the creature from Frankenstein.
