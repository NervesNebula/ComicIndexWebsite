CHILLLLLLL

CHILL comics can range from slice of life to high fantasy to sci-fi and even horror. The key difference here is that while they might not stray from the kinds of themes that NASTY comics have, if they do engage in those topics it probably won't be as explicit or heavy about it.

Summaries here will be written my me unless stated otherwise and may not reflect how the authors would describe their work.

Sakana by Mad Rupert



One of the funniest comics I've ever read. From the about page: "SAKANA is a slice-of-life, romantic comedy (a sli-li-rom-com, if you will) that takes place in the Tsukiji Fish Market in Tokyo, Japan. If follows the lives of a few 20-somethings working at the market, their friends and family, and all the wacky situations they constantly find themselves in despite their best efforts. Basically they all want to date each other, but they're all terrible at it, and also there's lots of seafood involved."

Tiger Tiger by Petra Erika Nordlund



From the about page: "Tiger, Tiger is a story of a young noble lady, who steals her brother's identity and his ship to sail across the world to find love and adventure, and to write a book about her favourite subject: the fascinating life cycle of sea sponges!"

The Rabbit Hole by Detrah



From the about page: "A group of rabbits routinely sacrifice themselves to a hole in the ground."

Monster Pulse By Magnolia



A group of children come together to figure out why parts of their bodies have suddenly become living breathing creatures.

The Property of Hate by Sarah Jolley



A story about a kid and a TV headed guy. About heroes, and stories, and what they're made of.

The Glass Scientists by Sage Cotugno



A queer retelling of Dr Jekyll and Mr Hyde.

Fantome-stein by Beka Duke



A retelling of the phantom of the opera, except the phantom is the creature from Frankenstein.

Shot and Chaser by E. K. Weaver



From the about page: "SHOT AND CHASER is a story about storms. Esoteric lifemates Tre and Olly set out together and experience a literal whirlwind of a day in the Texas panhandle."

TJ and Amal by E. K. Weaver



From the about page: "In the span of a single day, Amal calls off his arranged marriage, comes out to his conservative parents, promptly gets disowned, goes on a bender... and wakes up the next morning to find TJ, a lanky, dreadlocked vagrant, frying eggs and singing Paul Simon in his kitchen.

The agreement is simple: Amal does the driving; TJ pays the way - but a 3500 mile journey leaves plenty of time for things to get complicated."
